Shalini Potluri – Photography
"Photography taught me to see beauty in the simplest things."
I feel excited every time I discover a new angle to photograph.
Tip: Start small — just 10 minutes a day and keep experimenting.
David Patrick – Coding
“Coding is not about knowing everything — it’s about learning how to solve problems step by step.”
Coding makes me feel curious and challenged. Every error teaches me something new, and solving a problem gives a sense of achievement. It’s frustrating at times, but that’s what makes it rewarding.
Tip: Don’t be afraid of errors. Start with basics, practice daily, and learn by building small projects.
S. Arun – Pottery
“Working with clay reminds me that good things take time and patience.”
Pottery makes me feel calm and grounded. When I work with clay, I forget stress and focus completely on shaping something with my hands. Even mistakes become part of the final piece.
Tip: Start with simple shapes like bowls or cups. Let your hands learn slowly — clay responds best to patience.
